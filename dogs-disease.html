Dogs Diseases

Disease Symptom Remedy
Canine Coronavirus It is common in older dogs for there to be no symptoms Probiotics, fiber, fluids to prevent dehydration, and other treatments may be indicated depending on your dog's symptoms.
Canine Distemper High temperature, coughing, lethargy, loss of appetite, heavy breathing, red eyes and a runny nose. Diarrhoea and vomiting can also be symptoms. Treatment typically consists of supportive care and efforts to prevent secondary infections; control vomiting, diarrhea and neurologic symptoms; and combat dehydration through administration of fluids.
Canine Hepatitis High temperature, coughing, loss of appetite, depression, vomiting, pale gums and abdominal pain. Symptoms of jaundice may occur as this disease infects the liver and kidneys. treatment is aimed at managing the symptoms until the virus runs its course. Depending on the severity of illness, hospitalization and intravenous fluid therapy may be necessary.
Canine Influenza Coughing, sneezing, nasal discharge, breathing difficulties, variable fever, loss of appetite, and lethargy. Treatments are aimed at supporting affected dogs while their immune system fights the infection. Dogs that have complications (such as pneumonia or a bacterial infection at the same time) may need antibiotics or other medications.
Canine Parainfluenza A cough, a fever/temperature, nasal discharge, loss of appetite, and a lack of energy. Corrective recommendations for any environmental factors which are felt to be contributors. Cough suppressants which contain codeine derivatives should only be utilized for persistent non-productive cough control.
Leptospirosis High temperature, loss of appetite, vomiting, lethargy, depression, muscle pain, diarrhoea and blood in the urine. Leptospirosis is treatable with antibiotics. If an animal is treated early, it may recover more rapidly and any organ damage may be less severe. Other treatment methods, such as dialysis and hydration therapy may be required.
Parvovirus Loss of appetite, lethargy, persistent vomiting and bloody diarrhoea. Intravenous fluids (a drip) to treat shock and correct dehydration and electrolyte abnormalities.Anti-sickness medication.Painkillers.Plasma transfusions and/or blood transfusions to replace proteins and cells.
Rabies High temperature, seizures, paralysis, dropped jaw, inability to swallow, lack of coordination, shyness or aggression, frequent changes in behaviour and frothing at the mouth. If rabies is suspected, the dog has to be kept in isolation and prevented from escaping or injuring someone. "There is no treatment for a dog with rabies."
